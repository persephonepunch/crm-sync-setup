About the Service

CRM Sync exists to solve a specific problem: customer identity, consent, content, and commerce data are scattered across disconnected tools, and stitching them together is slow, brittle, and risky. The Service consolidates that work behind a single, server-side data layer so you configure once and operate across every channel. Its core capabilities include:

  • Configuration without glue code — connect Shopify, Webflow, Xano, Google Analytics, Adobe, and email or payment providers through guided setup, and manage everything from one place instead of maintaining bespoke, hand-built integrations.
  • Security by design — secrets are stored server-side and masked in read-backs, authentication fails closed, and credentials are rotated through an auditable, human-in-the-loop process; GDPR data-subject and deletion rails are built in.
  • Collaboration & handoff — the platform is multi-tenant and supports delegated, revocable access, so agencies, teams, and clients can work together and hand off projects without ever sharing master credentials.
  • Scaling at the edge — the Service runs on globally distributed edge infrastructure as independently deployable modules, so workloads scale and update without coupling or downtime.
  • Globalization — a first-class capability: multi-market and multi-locale storefronts, localized knowledge-base, FAQ, and product content, and machine translation (including edge-based translation) so you can reach customers in their own language and market.

These capabilities are provided as configurable tools; you remain responsible for how you set up, operate, and use them, as described below.

3.1 Access-Governance Model

The Service makes connected customer, consent, and commerce data accessible to your enterprise teams (product, compliance, analytics, agencies) and to authorized AI agents through a single governed gateway, so they can use that data without standing access to raw personal or payment data. These controls let you safely open data to more teams and agents — they define how access is granted, bounded, withdrawn, and evidenced, and are provided as tooling rather than as a managed-security service.

  • Least-privilege, scoped credentials: a root admin key (held by you), admin-equivalent rotatable delegate keys for time-bounded agency or consultant engagements, tenant-scoped crm_t_ tokens for delegated surfaces and client handoff, and read-only scoped keys limited to specific data classes. Issue the least-privileged credential sufficient for each recipient.
  • Key rotation & lifecycle: delegate keys rotate or revoke independently of the root secret and without service interruption, through an auditable, human-in-the-loop key-rotation ceremony; each credential is identifiable by a non-secret fingerprint and rotation count, and secrets never appear in logs, transcripts, or configuration read-backs.
  • Fail-closed & consent-gated: where a credential is absent or invalid, access is denied by default; access to end-user data is gated by the user’s explicit, versioned consent, which is monotonic (a withdrawal cannot be silently reversed) and recorded to an append-only audit trail. Agent capabilities follow consent automatically.
  • PII/PCI-free auditability: access and commerce events are logged free of personal and payment data, so compliance, security, and business teams can audit who accessed what, and under whose authority, without themselves being granted access to sensitive data.
